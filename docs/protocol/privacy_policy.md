## How We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

BladePipe's websites, softwares, online services, emails, and advertisements may use local storage technologies such as cookies and web beacons.

BladePipe treats information collected through cookies and similar technologies as non-personal information.

However, if local law considers IP addresses or similar identifiers to be personal information, BladePipe also treats such identifiers as personal information.

In addition, we sometimes combine non-personal information collected through these technologies with other personal information we hold. When we combine data in this way, we treat the combined data as personal information for the purposes of this policy.

### Cookies
A cookie is a plain text file that a web server stores on your computer or mobile device. The contents of a cookie can only be retrieved or read by the server that created it. Each cookie is unique to your web browser or mobile application. Cookies typically contain identifiers, site names, and some numbers and characters.

BladePipe sometimes stores cookies on your computer or mobile device, and we enable cookies to improve the user experience, including:

- Necessary Cookies
  - Log in and authenticate. When you log in to the website with your account, cookies allow you to jump directly from one page of the website to another without having to log in again on each page;
  - Store your preferences and settings. Cookies allow websites to save settings such as the language of your computer or mobile device, font size, shopping cart and other browsing preferences;
- Analytics Cookies
  - Statistical analysis. With the help of cookies, BladePipe may collect information about your use of our websites and other applications, such as recording a user's single visit (using session-based cookies) or multiple visits (using persistent cookies).
- Advertising Cookies
  - Interest-based advertising. BladePipe uses cookies to collect information about your online activities to discover your interests and to provide you with the most relevant advertising;

You can manage or delete cookies according to your preferences. For details, see AboutCookies.org. You can clear all cookies saved on your computer, and most web browsers have a feature to block cookies. For more information on how to change your browser settings, check with your browser provider.

If you clear cookies, you will need to change your user settings yourself each time you visit BladePipe's website. Please also note that some of BladePipe's services may require the use of cookies, and disabling cookies may affect all or part of your use of the functionality of those services.

### Web Beacons

In addition to cookies, we use other similar technologies, such as web beacons and pixel tags, on our websites to analyze service and product usage (combined with cookies) and to provide functions and services that are more relevant to your needs.

For example, the e-mails, installed softwares, and promotional articles sent to you may contain URLs that link to content on the BladePipe website. If you click on the link, we track the click to help us understand your product and service preferences and improve customer service.

### Do Not Track

Many web browsers have the Do Not Track feature, which posts Do Not Track requests to websites. Currently, the major Internet standards organizations do not have policies in place to dictate how websites should respond to such requests.

BladePipe does not currently support changing the handling of these cookies based on your chosen "Do Not Track" settings or other similar features.

## How You Manage Your Personal Information

In the countries/regions where BladePipe provides products and services, the legislation of some countries/regions stipulates the rights of personal data owners such as access, correction, deletion, and restriction of processing of personal information. In accordance with the requirements of local laws, the personal data owner or its agent may submit a request to BladePipe to exercise the above rights of personal data owners (hereinafter collectively referred to as the "Request").

### How to Make the Request

Personal data owner's requests must be made in writing. A request is valid even if the requester does not state the statute on which the request is based. Generally, an oral request for a visit is void, and BladePipe will comply with such provisions if the legislation of the local country also recognizes the validity of the oral request.

Personal data owner's requests can be submitted by contacting us, which is designed to protect the legitimate interests of personal data owners and protect the normal operation of BladePipe, and prevent the right to request personal information from being stolen or abused by others.

### Validity of the Request
Most laws require personal data owners to comply with specific requirements when making requests. This policy requires personal data owners to do the following:

- Provide BladePipe with sufficient information to verify their identity (to ensure that the person making the request is the owner of the personal information or a person authorized by the personal data owner);
- The content of the request is specific and enforceable.

### The Processing Period of the Request
BladePipe makes every effort to ensure that a response is made within one month after the personal data owner's request is submitted, and this period may be extended appropriately if necessary, given the complexity and volume of the request.

In the case of delay in providing information, BladePipe will notify the personal data owner of the relevant circumstances and the reason for the delay. In the event of a conflict between the time limits set forth in this paragraph and local legal provisions, local legal provisions shall prevail.

### The Result of the Request

After the personal data owner's requests are made, the following outcomes may occur:

1. The request is rejected

   In some cases, the personal data owner's request may be rejected, including but not limited to:
     - When local laws do not give the owner of personal information relevant rights;
     - When the identity of the person making the request cannot be verified;
     - When the request submitted by the personal data owner cannot be verified or exceeds the scope, especially when the request is repeatedly submitted;
     - If the information involved relates to compensation to be made or received by BladePipe in the dispute, and disclosure of the information is likely to harm BladePipe's interests;
     - If the information is retained only for statistical and research purposes and the publication of statistical and research results does not disclose the identity of the individual;
     - Other circumstances stipulated by law.

   If BladePipe rejects the personal data owner's request, it will formally explain the reason to the him/her.

2. The request succeeds

   When the circumstances in 1 do not occur, BladePipe will fulfill the request of the personal data owner. In order to increase the likelihood of a successful request, please provide as much detailed information as possible at the time of request, such as the type and specific content of the request, information about the data holder (such as the name of the BladePipe product and service you are using), the time frame in which the information was generated or processed (the smaller the time frame, the greater the likelihood of success), etc.
